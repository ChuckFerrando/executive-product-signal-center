# AI Baseline Shift — Judgment Signal

## Source
Eoin O'Toole (Riviera Partners) — Sidebar session on AI hiring (2026)

## Signal Summary

AI has raised the execution baseline across all candidates, making AI fluency a non-differentiating skill. Hiring evaluation has shifted toward judgment, decision-making, and product-level thinking.

## Key Observations

- AI usage is now assumed; it is no longer a differentiator
- Candidates over-index on:
  - tools
  - speed
  - output
- Hiring managers are evaluating:
  - what candidates do after AI produces an answer
  - how candidates apply judgment, ethics, and customer considerations
- Interviews increasingly include:
  - real-time capability validation (e.g., live building, “vibe coding”)
- Product leaders are expected to understand:
  - model selection
  - system structure (product-level, not engineering-level)
  - control layers
  - connection to user outcomes
- Candidates who cannot demonstrate real understanding are quickly exposed

## Signal Implications

- Execution is commoditized; judgment is the differentiator
- AI fluency must be paired with decision-making capability
- Product leaders must operate at the system level, not just feature level
- Interview performance depends on real capability, not narrative

## Classification

- Type: Hiring Signal
- Domain: AI Product Leadership
- Strength: High
- Actionability: High
